Nuage À Plots" is a shelving unit designed by Charlotte Perriand in 1952 for the Italian furniture company, Cassina. The name translates to "Cloud with Rods" in English.

The shelving unit consists of a series of modular, asymmetrical, and interlocking shelves supported by vertical metal rods, creating an open, airy structure reminiscent of a cloud. The rods are not only functional but also serve as a decorative element, giving the shelving unit an elegant and light appearance.

Perriand's design was innovative for its time, as it offered a flexible and adaptable storage solution that could be easily reconfigured to suit different spaces and storage needs. The unit's asymmetrical design allows for the shelves to be arranged in a variety of configurations, creating an interesting visual effect and enabling it to fit into a range of environments.

The Nuage À Plots shelving unit is still in production today and is considered a classic of mid-century modern design. It is a testament to Perriand's design skills and her ability to create functional yet visually striking furniture that remains popular and relevant over half a century later.
