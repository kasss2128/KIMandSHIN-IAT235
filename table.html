The Free-form Dining Table designed by Charlotte Perriand is a unique and innovative piece of furniture that reflects Perriand's interest in combining organic forms with modernist design principles.

The table's irregular shape, with its rounded edges and angled corners, gives it a distinctive and fluid appearance, while the use of natural wood gives it a warm and inviting feel.

Ledges and skylights invite natural light from the seams of the ceiling and shine onto the artists’ works. The strategic use of space and light creates cohesion between the artwork and the concrete walls, as if they are threaded together by the light.

The table's legs are another standout feature, with their angled design and black-painted metal feet providing a sense of stability and modernity. The three legs are positioned to create an asymmetric form that adds visual interest to the overall design.
