Jean Prouvé's Dactylo desk is a piece of modernist furniture designed by the French designer and architect Jean Prouvé in 1953. The desk was originally designed for the offices of the French Communist Party, but has since become an icon of mid-century modern design.

The Dactylo desk is notable for its minimalist design and functional features. The desk is constructed from folded and welded sheet steel, with a top made of melamine-faced chipboard. The desk features a single drawer, which can be locked in place with a key, and a small shelf underneath the top surface for storing papers and other items.

One of the key design elements of the Dactylo desk is its cantilevered design. The desk's legs are angled outwards, providing stability and support for the top surface, while also creating a sense of lightness and visual interest.

— Jean Prouve

The Dactylo desk was designed with the needs of modern office workers in mind. Prouvé believed that furniture should be both functional and aesthetically pleasing, and the Dactylo desk is a perfect example of this philosophy. The desk's simple, elegant design makes it an ideal choice for a wide range of office environments, from home offices to corporate workspaces.

Overall, the Dactylo desk is a classic example of modernist design, combining form and function in a way that is both beautiful and practical. Its clean lines and minimalist aesthetic have made it a popular choice for design enthusiasts and collectors around the world.
