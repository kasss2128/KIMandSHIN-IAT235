Design made Simple

Jean Prouvé's designs were known for their simplicity, functionality, and industrial aesthetic, reflecting his background in metalworking and engineering. He believed that design should serve a practical purpose and that beautiful and well-made objects could be produced using industrial manufacturing techniques.

This exhibition aims to display Prouvé's masterful use of matrial, light and primitive shapes to create spaces that exude the Shinto concept of "emptiness".
